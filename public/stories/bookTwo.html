The Journey of Father Abraham
“A friend of God.
A father of nations.
Long ago, in the ancient city of Ur, there lived a man named Abram. He was the son of Terah, a descendant of Shem, son of Noah. Life in Ur was full of riches and idols, but Abram’s heart was different. One night, a voice called out from the heavens. “Leave your country, your people, and your father’s household. Go to the land I will show you. I will make you into a great nation… and through you, all the families of the earth will be blessed.” Though he was seventy-five years old, Abram believed the voice. He packed up his tents, took his wife Sarai, his nephew Lot, and all their possessions, and set off for a land he had never seen—Canaan.
In Canaan, Abram built altars and called on the name of the Lord. But soon, famine struck the land. Abram journeyed to Egypt, where he feared for his life. Sarai, his wife, was beautiful, and he worried Pharaoh would kill him to take her. So he lied: “She is my sister.” Pharaoh took Sarai, but plagues struck his household. When the truth was revealed, Pharaoh rebuked Abram and sent them away. Back in Canaan, tensions rose between Abram’s herdsmen and Lot’s. Abram, seeking peace, let Lot choose his land first. Lot chose the lush valley of Sodom, while Abram remained in the hill country. God appeared again and said, “Look around, Abram. All the land you see—I will give it to you and your offspring forever.”
One dark night, God took Abram outside and said, “Look at the stars. Can you count them? So shall your descendants be.” Though he had no children, Abram believed, and God counted it as righteousness. God made a covenant with him—a sacred promise. He changed his name from Abram (“exalted father”) to Abraham (“father of many”), and Sarai became Sarah (“princess”). To seal the promise, God commanded Abraham to be circumcised, marking his family as chosen forever.
Years passed. Sarah remained childless, and doubt crept in. So she gave her Egyptian maid, Hagar, to Abraham. Hagar bore a son, Ishmael. But this was not the promised child. One day, three mysterious visitors appeared at Abraham’s tent. He welcomed them with a feast. They said, “This time next year, Sarah will have a son.” Sarah laughed—she was ninety years old! But God said, “Is anything too hard for the Lord?” And so, just as promised, Isaac was born. His name meant “laughter.”
Years later, God gave Abraham a test that shook the heavens. “Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and sacrifice him on a mountain I will show you.” With a heavy heart, Abraham obeyed. He laid wood on Isaac’s back, climbed the mountain, and built the altar. Just as he raised the knife— “Stop!” cried an angel. “Now I know you fear God.” A ram was caught in the thicket. Isaac was spared. God reaffirmed the promise: “Through your offspring, all nations on earth will be blessed.”
Sarah died at 127, and Abraham mourned deeply. He purchased the Cave of Machpelah as a burial site—the first piece of the Promised Land he ever owned. He later arranged a wife, Rebekah, for Isaac from his homeland. Peace settled in his heart. At the age of 175, Abraham died, full of years and faith. He was buried beside Sarah. 🌟 Legacy: Father of Nations From Isaac came Jacob, who became Israel. From Israel came a great nation. And through that nation, Jesus, the Messiah. Today, Abraham is revered by Jews, Christians, and Muslims as the father of faith, a man who walked with God into the unknown—and found the promise fulfilled. “Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness.” – Romans 4:3
