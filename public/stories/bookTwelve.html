Percy Jackson
Twelve-year-old Percy Jackson never felt normal. Strange things always happened around him — things he couldn’t explain. Pens turned into swords. Water seemed to obey him. And monsters kept showing up in the most ridiculous places … even in class. But the day his algebra teacher turned into a winged demon and attacked him, Percy realized something huge: He wasn’t just a troubled kid — he was the son of a Greek god. 🌊 A World He Never Knew Percy discovered that the ancient Greek gods — Zeus, Poseidon, Hades and the rest — were still alive, hidden in the modern world. Monsters disguised themselves as teachers, tourists, and even neighbors. Heroes trained in a secret place called Camp Half-Blood. And Percy? He was a demigod — half human, half god. His father was Poseidon, the ruler of the seas. This made Percy extremely powerful … and extremely hunted. ⚡ The Lightning Thief Before Percy could even adjust to this new world, disaster struck. Zeus’s master lightning bolt — the most powerful weapon in existence — had been stolen. And Zeus believed only one person could have taken it: Percy Jackson. Suddenly, the entire supernatural world wanted Percy captured … or killed. But the truth was even worse: If the bolt wasn’t returned, the gods would go to war — a war big enough to destroy the entire earth. 🛡 The Quest Begins To clear his name and stop a divine war, Percy accepted a dangerous quest. His allies: Annabeth Chase – daughter of Athena, brilliant and deadly. Grover Underwood – Percy’s best friend and a loyal satyr protector. Together, the trio journeyed across America, where every city hid a monster from ancient Greek myths. They battled: The Furies, screaming for Percy’s soul Medusa, whose eyes could turn flesh into stone A Chimera, breathing fire on top of the St. Louis Arch A deadly Lotus-eater trap designed to make them forget their mission Every moment felt like walking through a nightmare with gods watching from above. 🔥 The Underworld Showdown Clues led Percy to the Underworld, where Hades ruled. They crossed the field of eternal souls and rivers of fire, searching for the bolt. But something was wrong. Hades had no bolt. In fact, his magical helm had also been stolen. Someone was manipulating all the gods — setting them against each other. And then Percy found the stolen Lightning Bolt in the one place he never expected: Inside his own backpack — placed there without his knowledge. He had been framed. 🐍 The Real Enemy Appears Back on the surface, Percy discovered the mastermind: Luke Castellan, a powerful son of Hermes. Luke hated the gods and wanted them destroyed. He planned to use gods against gods until Olympus fell apart. He summoned a deadly, venomous scorpion to kill Percy. But Percy survived. He returned the bolt to Zeus, preventing the gods from erupting into war. Olympus was saved — for now. ⚡ A Hero Is Born Percy came back to Camp Half-Blood stronger, braver, and more confident. He understood something important: The prophecy about him had only begun. Bigger threats were rising. The Titan lord Kronos was awakening. And Percy—stormborn son of Poseidon—was destined to face him one day. His journey was far from over. This was only the beginning…
