The Story Of David And Goliath
The Israelites and the Philistines were at war, standing on two opposite hills with a valley between them. Every day for forty days, a giant Philistine warrior named Goliath came forward to challenge the Israelites. He was over nine feet tall and heavily armed, and his booming voice frightened the entire Israelite army. Goliath demanded that one Israelite man fight him, but no one had the courage to face him. At this time, a young shepherd named David visited the battlefield to bring food to his brothers who were soldiers. When David heard Goliath insulting Israel and mocking God, he became angry that no one had confronted the giant. Although he was not a soldier, David volunteered to fight Goliath. King Saul tried to give him armor, but it was too heavy, so David chose to fight with his sling and five smooth stones—just as he had defended his father’s sheep from lions and bears. When David approached Goliath, the giant mocked him. But David declared that he came not with weapons, but in the name of the Lord. David placed a stone in his sling, swung it, and released it with great speed. The stone struck Goliath on the forehead, and the giant fell to the ground. David then used Goliath’s own sword to finish him. The Philistines fled in fear, and the Israelites won a great victory.
