The Book Of Enoch
Long before the Great Flood, before Noah built his ark, and before humanity understood the mysteries of heaven, there lived a man named Enoch—a man so righteous, so pure, that he “walked with God.” But his life was not ordinary. He was chosen to witness secrets no human had ever seen. This is his story. 🌩 The Watchers and Their Forbidden Descent In the earliest days, a group of powerful angels called The Watchers were sent to earth to guide and watch over humanity. They were majestic, glowing beings—tall as mountains, with voices like thunder. But they made a dangerous decision. Under the leadership of Shemhazah, 200 Watchers stood on Mount Hermon and swore an oath: “We will descend to earth, take human wives, and create a new generation.” Their beauty and power seduced the daughters of men. But from this union came giants—the Nephilim—creatures so huge and strong that they shook the earth when they walked. The Nephilim became violent, devouring animals, crops, and even humans. Their corruption spread like poison. Heaven was no longer silent. ⚡ Enoch’s Calling God saw the corruption and chose Enoch as the messenger between heaven and the fallen angels. One night, as Enoch slept, a blinding light filled his room. Angels lifted him into the sky without wings, carrying him through layers of heaven—each more radiant than the last. He was shown: ✨ The storehouses of the winds ✨ The paths of the stars ✨ The spirits guarding creation ✨ The future judgment of the world And then he stood before God’s Throne, surrounded by angelic fire. God said: “Go to the Watchers and deliver My judgment.” Enoch trembled, but obeyed. 🔥 Enoch Confronts the Fallen Angels The Watchers—once glorious—now appeared desperate and broken. They begged Enoch: “Please ask God to forgive us. We regret what we have done.” Enoch went away to pray for them. But when he returned with God’s message, the answer was terrifying: “There will be no forgiveness. Your children—the Nephilim—will be destroyed. And you, Watchers, will be imprisoned until the Day of Judgment.” The Watchers cried, their voices shaking mountains. They knew their fate was sealed. 🌊 The Coming of the Flood God revealed to Enoch that the earth would be cleansed. The Nephilim would fall in battle, killing each other in violent wars. Their spirits would remain on earth as wicked demons, roaming restlessly. Only one family would survive the coming destruction—Noah, Enoch’s descendant. Enoch wrote everything in heavenly books and taught his son Methuselah to preserve the knowledge. 👑 Enoch’s Final Journey After completing his mission, something miraculous happened. Enoch was walking one evening when a brilliant pathway of light opened in the sky. Angels appeared and said: “The Lord calls you. Come.” Enoch stepped onto the light—and he never returned. The Bible says simply: “Enoch walked with God, and he was not, for God took him.” But the Book of Enoch reveals that he was transformed into a heavenly being—forever dwelling among the angels as a scribe of divine mysteries.
