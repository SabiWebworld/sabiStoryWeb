Sodom and Gomorrah
Long ago, in the days of Abraham, there were two cities called Sodom and Gomorrah. These cities were located in a fertile valley near the Jordan River. At first glance, the land looked beautiful—green fields, flowing water, and busy streets. But inside the cities, the people were known for wickedness. They treated strangers badly, practiced great evil, and had no respect for God or for one another. God saw how corrupt the cities had become. Their wickedness was so great that God decided to judge them. But before destroying the cities, God chose to reveal His plan to Abraham, His faithful servant. Abraham’s nephew, Lot, lived in Sodom, so Abraham was worried for him. When God told Abraham what would happen, Abraham boldly pleaded for the cities. He asked: “Lord, if there are righteous people in the city, will You destroy them too?” God agreed that if He found even 10 righteous people, He would spare the whole city. But sadly, the cities were so filled with evil that not even 10 good people could be found. That evening, two angels arrived in Sodom disguised as men. Lot saw them at the city gate and immediately welcomed them into his home. He knew the city was dangerous, especially for strangers. Lot prepared a meal for them, but before they could rest, the men of Sodom surrounded the house. They demanded that Lot hand over the visitors so they could harm them. Lot begged the crowd to stop, but they refused. Just as the mob rushed forward, the angels reached out, pulled Lot inside, and struck the men outside with blindness so they could not find the door. The angels then told Lot: “Gather your family. Leave this city now! God is going to destroy it.” Lot tried to warn his sons-in-law, but they laughed, thinking he was joking. Early the next morning, the angels urged Lot, his wife, and his two daughters to escape quickly. The city’s destruction was moments away. As they fled, the angels gave a strict warning: “Do not look back.” Lot and his daughters obeyed, but his wife, hearing the screams and seeing the fire behind her, turned around. The moment she looked back, she became a pillar of salt—frozen by her disobedience. As the family reached safety, God sent down fire and brimstone from the sky. Flames consumed Sodom and Gomorrah completely. The green valley turned into a smoking wasteland. Only Lot and his two daughters survived. The story ended as a warning for all generations: God is patient and merciful, but He is also just. Evil cannot continue forever.
