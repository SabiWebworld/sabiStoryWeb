The Story Of Muhammed The First Muslim
Many centuries ago in the city of Mecca, in Arabia, a man was born who would change the world forever. His name was Muhammad, son of Abdullah. He belonged to the respected Quraysh tribe, but he grew up without his parents—his father died before he was born and his mother died when he was very young. Muhammad was raised by his grandfather and later by his uncle Abu Talib. Even as a young man, Muhammad was known for his honesty, kindness, and wisdom. People called him “Al-Amin,” meaning the Trustworthy, because he never lied and always kept his word. Muhammad Before Prophethood Muhammad worked as a merchant and gained a reputation for fairness in business. A wealthy widow named Khadijah hired him to manage her trade caravan. She was impressed by his character, and they later married. Their marriage was peaceful and full of respect. But even with success, Muhammad disliked the corruption and idol worship in Mecca. He often went to a cave called Hira, on Mount Nur, to meditate and pray for guidance. The First Revelation One night—known as the Night of Power (Laylat al-Qadr)—something extraordinary happened. While Muhammad was alone in the cave, the Angel Jibril (Gabriel) suddenly appeared and said, “Read!” (Iqra’) Muhammad replied that he could not read. But the angel repeated the command three times, and then revealed the first verses of the Qur’an: “Read in the name of your Lord who created…” Muhammad felt overwhelmed and rushed home to Khadijah, trembling. She comforted him and assured him that God would never abandon a man as noble as he was. She became the first person to believe in his message — making her the first Muslim. The Beginning of Islam God continued to send revelations to Muhammad through Angel Jibril. Muhammad learned that there was only one God (Allah) and that people were to worship Him alone, live righteously, and treat others with justice. Muhammad began teaching quietly among his family and close friends. Among the earliest Muslims were: Khadijah Abu Bakr Ali ibn Abi Talib Zayd ibn Harithah As the message spread, more people accepted Islam.
