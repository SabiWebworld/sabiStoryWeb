The Children of rapture
The world changes the day of the Rapture.
A bright light sweeps across the earth, millions vanish, and silence replaces normal life. But a few children are left behind — not ordinary children, but kids marked with glowing symbols on their wrists. These children gain strange powers and are called the Children of Rapture. Eliora, a girl whose mother disappears during the Rapture, wakes with the Mark of Fire. That night she dreams of a heavenly messenger telling her to find the other marked children because a great darkness has escaped into the world. As the days pass, sunlight begins to fade earlier every day. Night grows stronger. Demons and shadow-creatures roam freely. The world fears the marked children, but prophecy speaks of them as protectors. Following a mysterious pull, Eliora meets Kael, the boy with the Mark of Winter, who can control freezing wind. The two defeat a shadow creature together and realize they are being hunted by a powerful evil force called the Devourer, a being that escaped when heaven opened. Soon after, they save a little girl named Suri, who has the Mark of Sound, able to create shockwaves with her voice. Eliora teaches her to use her power, proving that every marked child is meant for something greater. Together, the three children set out to find the remaining thirteen marked children, because only united can they stop the Devourer. The darkness grows stronger each day, twisting the sky and haunting every step they take. In the final moment, the sky forms a terrible face of shadow, and the Devourer speaks: “Children of Rapture… I am coming.” Knowing time is running out, Eliora, Kael, and Suri begin their journey to gather the rest of the chosen children and fight the rising darkness — the last hope for a broken world.
