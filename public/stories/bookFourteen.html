The Great Gatsby
In the summer of 1922, on the glittering shores of Long Island, a young man named Nick Carraway moved into a small rented cottage. His neighbors were nothing like him. On one side were the wealthy, polished families of East Egg. On the other side stood a mysterious mansion that came alive every night with fireworks, music, and champagne rivers. That mansion belonged to Jay Gatsby — a man no one really knew, but everyone talked about. 🎉 A Mansion of Secrets Every weekend, Gatsby’s house exploded into life. Jazz bands played till dawn. Rum flowed like water. Lavish guests—none of whom had been personally invited—filled the evenings with dancing, rumors, and sparkling chaos. Some said Gatsby was a prince. Some said he killed a man. Others whispered he was a spy, a smuggler, or a magician. But no one actually knew the truth. Except Gatsby himself. ❤ A Love Buried in the Past Nick soon learned the real reason behind Gatsby’s parties: Not for fame. Not for fun. But for one woman. Daisy Buchanan — Nick’s cousin, who lived across the bay with her wealthy husband, Tom. Before the war, Gatsby and Daisy had fallen deeply in love. But while Gatsby was fighting overseas, Daisy married Tom, a man of money, power, and arrogance. Gatsby had spent years building his fortune and his mansion, hoping one day Daisy would drift into one of his parties. Gatsby believed: “You can repeat the past. You can rewrite everything.” His heart lived in the moment he met Daisy… and he was determined to win her back. 🌙 The Reunion Nick arranged a quiet meeting between the two former lovers. Daisy trembled. Gatsby nearly broke down from nervousness. But when they saw each other again, the years melted away. Their connection reignited—bright, dangerous, and unstoppable. Soon they were sneaking around behind Tom’s back. Late-night drives. Secret afternoons. Whispers of running away together. Gatsby thought his dream was finally coming true. But dreams built on illusions are fragile. 🔥 Confrontation Tom Buchanan eventually discovered the affair. In a tense, heated scene at a New York hotel, Tom struck at Gatsby with the cruelest truth: Daisy would never leave the comfort, money, and stability Tom provided. And Daisy, overwhelmed and frightened, stepped back into her husband’s shadow. Gatsby’s dream shattered—but he refused to accept it. He loved Daisy too deeply. 🚗 A Tragic Accident As Daisy and Gatsby drove home that night, tragedy struck. A woman named Myrtle—Tom’s secret lover—ran into the road. The car hit her. She died instantly. Daisy had been driving. But Gatsby, blinded by love, decided to take the blame. “I’ll protect her,” he told Nick. “I’ll take responsibility for everything.” That decision sealed his fate. 💔 The Fall of Gatsby Myrtle’s husband, George, was driven mad by grief and deception. Tom, eager to protect his own secrets, told George that Gatsby’s car had killed Myrtle. George went to Gatsby’s mansion. On a quiet afternoon, while Gatsby stood alone in his pool, George shot him. Then turned the gun on himself. Just like that— the man who chased the American Dream, the man who filled nights with wonder and magic, the man who loved too deeply— was gone. 🕊 Only One Person Came When Gatsby died, the crowds vanished. All those people who drank his wine, danced in his halls, and bathed in his luxury—none of them showed up for his funeral. Only Nick, broken-hearted, stood for him. Nick realized that Gatsby, despite his flaws, had something the others lacked: A dream. A hope. A belief in something greater. And that’s why Nick left New York, whispering words that would echo across generations: “Gatsby believed in the green light… and in the future we never truly reach.”
